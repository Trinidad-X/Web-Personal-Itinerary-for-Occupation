Your portfolio is your proof of work. It matters more than your GPA.

Essential Sections:

  • Projects: Cards with screenshots, tech stack icons, live links, and GitHub links.

Landing Internships

Experience trumps theory. Getting the first one is the hardest.

Application Pipeline 75%

Cold Message Template (LinkedIn)

Don't just apply. Network.

Hi [Name], I'm a CS student at [University] passionate about [Topic]. I've been following [Company]'s work on [Specific Project] and built a similar project recently: [Link]. I'd love to ask two quick questions about your engineering culture if you have 5 mins. Best, [Your Name]

Where to Apply

  • LinkedIn Jobs: Filter by "Internship" & "Past 24 hours".
  • Levels.fyi: For salary transparency.
  • Y Combinator "Work at a Startup": Great for startup roles.

Resume Architecture

Use the XYZ Formula: "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]".

ATS Readiness 90%

The STAR Method

Use this for bullet points and behavioral interviews:

Situation Describe the context/problem.
Task What was your responsibility?
Action What specific steps did you take? (Use "I", not "We")
Result Quantifiable outcome (e.g., "Reduced latency by 20%").

Formatting Rules

  • Keep it One Page (unless 10+ years experience).
  • Export as PDF, never Word.
  • Use a single column layout for better ATS parsing.

Soft Skills & EQ

Hireable engineers are pleasant to work with. Don't be a "brilliant jerk".

Communication 95%

During the Interview

  • Think Out Loud: Never code in silence. Explain your thought process.
  • Clarify Requirements: Ask questions before you start coding.
  • Admit Unknowns: It's okay to say "I don't know, but here is how I would find out."

Negotiation Tips

Always negotiate. Even a 5% increase compounds over years.

  • Don't give a number first.
  • Counter-offer based on market data (Levels.fyi).
  • Consider equity and sign-on bonuses if base salary is fixed.
