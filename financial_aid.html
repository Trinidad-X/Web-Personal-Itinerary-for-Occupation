FAFSA Basics

FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) is the first step to receive federal grants, loans, and work-study eligibility.

  • Open to US citizens and eligible non-citizens
  • Submitted online annually
  • Determines Expected Family Contribution (EFC)

Grants and Tuition Waivers

Grants are non-repayable aid; tuition waivers reduce or eliminate fees.

  • Pell Grant: need-based federal grant
  • State grants: vary by state and program
  • Merit-based waivers for academic or athletic achievement

Work-Study and Part-Time Work

Work-study programs allow students to earn income while gaining relevant experience.

  • On-campus or partner organizations
  • Flexible hours to accommodate class schedules
  • Part-time jobs also supplement living costs

Budgeting & Cost of Living

Effective budgeting helps manage tuition, rent, food, transport, and personal expenses.

  • Track monthly expenses
  • Plan for textbooks and software tools
  • Consider shared housing or meal plans
